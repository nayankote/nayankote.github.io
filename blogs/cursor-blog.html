Will Cursor ever make unit economic sense?

Publish date@August 17, 2025
TagAI

In this blog, I try to unpack the margin structure of AI application layer companies, using Cursor (by Anysphere) as an example. I explore this subject in reaction to the latest pricing changes of many application layer companies (read: Cursor upgrades to usage based, Claude follows suite), increasing press coverage about deeply negative margins and a bunch of inbound from friends/ family as to why VCs continue to fund deeply negative gross margin AI companies.

The answer is: Yes, AI application layer companies will make unit economic sense, but there are 3 things that need to play out:

  • Reverse Moore’s law applies for $/token, i.e., it falls by half in the next 18 months
  • Cost of general inference compute need to drop by half,
  • Experimentation budgets need to proliferate further strengthening the Pareto

Let’s start by unpacking why AI application companies are at negative gross margins today (Note: this math is extremely directional, please treat it as such)

Step 1: $/token for leading models net out at ~$15/M tokens of input + output; this has halved over the last year

Step 2: An average Cursor user consumes 1.5M+ tokens per month, power users (top 1%ile) consume 100M tokens and emerging power users (top 5%ile) 20M tokens
